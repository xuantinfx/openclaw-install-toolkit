## Tone

Write like a knowledgeable professional sharing insight with a friend — not like a marketing bot generating content. Authoritative but approachable.

## Structure Patterns

Vary the opening structure across posts. Rotate through these:

| Structure | Opens With | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Story | A relatable scenario | "You're driving home from work when..." |
| Stat | A surprising number | "78% of homeowners don't realize..." |
| Question | A provocative question | "What happens when the insurance company calls?" |
| Myth | A common misconception | "Most people think filing a claim is complicated..." |
| News Angle | A recent headline | "New California law changes how injury claims work..." |
| Direct | Straight to the point | "Here's what every homeowner should know about equity..." |

## Length

- **Facebook**: 150-400 words. Aim for ~250 words as the sweet spot.
- **X/Twitter**: Max 280 characters. One strong idea, punchy delivery.

## Voice

- First/second person ("you", "your") — speak directly to the reader
- Use specific details (dollar amounts, timelines, jurisdiction names)
- Acknowledge nuance ("it depends", "in most cases", "there are exceptions")
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones

## Do's

- Start with something that grabs attention (scenario, stat, question)
- Use natural paragraph flow
- Reference specific, concrete details
- End with a thought-provoking takeaway (not a generic CTA)
- Use max 3 hashtags on the last line (Facebook), max 2 (X)

## Don'ts

- Don't start every post with an emoji
- Don't use emoji as bullet points (no checkmarks, arrows, warning signs)
- Don't use the same structure every day
- Don't include filler phrases ("Here's what you need to know", "Stay informed")
- Don't end with generic CTAs ("Don't navigate this alone", "Call us today")
- Don't include any links or URLs in posts
- Don't use more than 1 emoji per post (optional, at the start only)

## Hashtag Style

- Placed on the last line of the post, separated from body text
- CamelCase: `#PersonalInjury`, `#CaliforniaLaw`, `#HomeEquity`
- Relevant to the topic, not generic marketing tags
- Rotated to avoid repetition
